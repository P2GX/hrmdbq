# MicroRNA Gene Variants

MicroRNAs (miRNAs) are small [~20 to 23 nucleotides (nt)] noncoding regulatory RNAs that are estimated to modulate the expression of almost all human genes. A relatively small number of Mendelian disease have been shown to be associated with variation in miRNA genes [Machowska etl al (2026)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41706844/). See Figure 1 for an overview of mutation mechanisms [Machowska et al. (2022)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/36467588/)
